FAQ

Common questions.

Is this therapy or sports psychology?+
No. Mental Performance Coaching is not therapy and does not deal with clinical or psychological issues. It's practical, forward-focused work on specific mental skills that affect your performance — focus, confidence, pressure management, emotional control. Think of it as training for your mind, the same way you train your body.
How does a session work?+
Sessions are 60 minutes, and can be conducted online via video call — so it doesn't matter where you are in the world. We work on specific mental skills, review what's happening in your performances, and build practical tools you can apply immediately in training and competition. Between sessions, you put the work into practice.
Is everything we discuss confidential?+
Completely. Everything discussed in our sessions stays between us. Nothing is shared with coaches, clubs, parents, or anyone else — unless you explicitly ask me to. Confidentiality is the foundation of this work.
How quickly will I see results?+
Most athletes notice a shift in awareness and clarity within the first two or three sessions. Lasting change — where new mental habits become automatic under pressure — typically takes the full package. That's why a single session isn't offered: one conversation starts a process, it doesn't complete one.
Do I need to be a professional or elite athlete?+
Not at all. This work is for any athlete who is serious about their performance — regardless of age, gender, sport, or level. If you're stuck at a plateau, keep making the same mistakes under pressure, or feel like your mental game is holding your physical game back, this is for you.
Online or in person?+
Both is possible. Online sessions make it possible to work with athletes anywhere in the world, without travel or scheduling constraints. Video call tools like Zoom or Teams work perfectly for this kind of work.
